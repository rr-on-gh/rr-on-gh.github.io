On-device automatic meeting transcripts.

Meeting Transcriptor quietly lives in your menu bar and starts a transcript the moment you join a Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, or Slack huddle call. When you leave, it stops and saves the file. Nothing to click each time. Nothing leaves your Mac.

What you get

  • Fully local

    Transcription runs on your Mac using the Apple Neural Engine. Audio never leaves the machine. Network is used once, for the initial model download.

  • Auto-start for meetings

    Works with Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Google Meet, and Slack huddles. Opt-in once; transcription starts the instant a call begins and stops automatically when it ends.

  • Lives in the menu bar

    A small icon at the top of your screen shows when a session is active. Click for the current file, reveal in Finder, or the transcripts folder.

How it works

Turn on auto-start once in the menu. From that point on, Meeting Transcriptor watches for meetings across the apps you already use and handles the rest.

01

You join a call

Click your usual meeting link. No extra step, no bot invited to the call, no prompt in the middle of the conversation.

02

Transcription starts

A quiet notification confirms Meeting Transcriptor is running. The menu bar icon switches to active and a new transcript file is created for this session.

03

You leave, it saves

A few seconds after the call ends, the session closes and the file is finalized. Open it in Finder, paste it into notes, or hand it to your own follow-up workflow.

Install

  1. 01

    Open the DMG

    Double-click the downloaded MeetingTranscriptor.dmg, then drag Meeting Transcriptor.app into your Applications folder.

  2. 02

    Grant three permissions

    On first launch macOS will ask for Microphone, Speech Recognition, and Screen & System Audio Recording. After granting the screen recording permission, fully quit the app (menu → Quit) and reopen it so macOS picks up the grant.

  3. 03

    Turn on auto-start

    Click the mic icon in the menu bar and toggle Auto-start for Zoom / Teams / Meet. First run downloads the transcription model (~632 MB) and prepares it for the Apple Neural Engine, which takes 3–8 minutes. Every launch after that starts in seconds.

Recommended

Use headphones during meetings. External speakers cause your mic to pick up the remote side, which produces duplicated lines tagged as both You and Them. Headphones keep the split clean.
